When everything feels urgent, urgency itself stops being useful. The label becomes noise because it no longer distinguishes what will actually create loss if delayed.

A better approach is to look for asymmetry. Which item gets materially worse if it sits another day? Which one closes a decision, removes a blocker, or protects a relationship? That is where urgency becomes real instead of emotional.
