An AI chief of staff should not just answer questions. It should help manage priorities, detect what is slipping, and prepare work that pushes real outcomes forward. If it only reacts when asked, it behaves more like a chatbot than a chief of staff.

The role is less about conversation and more about judgment. What changed, what is getting stuck, what needs pressure, and what can be ignored. Those are the problems that create executive drag.
