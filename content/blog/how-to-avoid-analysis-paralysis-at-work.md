Analysis paralysis happens when the cost of choosing feels higher than the cost of delaying. You keep collecting context, comparing options, or rethinking the same problem because commitment feels riskier than waiting.

What helps is making the next move smaller and clearer. The more directly an action changes the board, the easier it is to stop spinning and start moving.
