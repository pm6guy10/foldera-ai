Context switching is expensive because each switch forces you to reload goals, timelines, people, and unfinished thoughts. Even short switches have a hidden tax if they happen often enough.

That tax gets worse when your systems are fragmented. Email, tasks, decisions, and follow ups all live in separate places, which means the burden of reconnection falls back on your memory.
